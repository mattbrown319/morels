Data Sources

  • Morel sightings & indicator species — iNaturalist, a joint initiative of the California Academy of Sciences and the National Geographic Society. Observation data used under individual Creative Commons licenses.
  • Soil temperature & precipitation — Open-Meteo weather API. Data: ERA5 (Copernicus/ECMWF). Licensed under CC BY 4.0.
  • Ecoregion classification — RESOLVE Ecoregions 2017. CC BY 4.0.
  • Public land boundaries — MassGIS, Massachusetts Executive Office of Energy and Environmental Affairs.
  • Forest cover — National Land Cover Database (NLCD), Multi-Resolution Land Characteristics Consortium.
  • Base map — CARTO & OpenStreetMap contributors.

Methodology

The morel probability score is derived from analysis of 49,000+ iNaturalist observations correlated with historical weather data. Key validated findings:

  • Soil temperature sweet spot: 50–61°F (10–16°C) at 6cm depth
  • Precipitation: ~1.5" of rain in the 14 days before fruiting
  • Indicator species co-occurrence validated against published mycological research

Honorable Harvest
